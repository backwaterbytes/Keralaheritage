Kerala Timeline

Journey Through Kerala's History

Discover the rich heritage and cultural evolution of God's Own Country

500 BCE

The Chera Dynasty Begins

The ancient Chera dynasty establishes its rule over Kerala, marking the beginning of organized governance and trade relations with distant lands including Rome and China.

52 CE

Arrival of St. Thomas

St. Thomas the Apostle arrives in Kerala, introducing Christianity to the region. This marks the beginning of Kerala's diverse religious landscape that continues today.

9th Century

Golden Age of Arts

The Chera Perumal kingdom at Mahodayapuram flourishes, becoming a center for temple architecture, classical arts, and Malayalam literature development.

12th Century

Birth of Martial Arts

Kalaripayattu, the ancient martial art form, reaches its peak development. The roots of Kathakali dance-drama also begin to emerge during this period.

14th Century

Spice Trade Flourishes

Arab and Chinese traders establish strong commercial ties with Kozhikode. The famous Chinese fishing nets are introduced, becoming an iconic symbol of Kerala.

1498

Vasco da Gama's Arrival

Portuguese explorer Vasco da Gama lands in Calicut, opening the sea route to India and beginning the European colonial influence in Kerala.

1663

Dutch Colonial Period

The Dutch East India Company expands control over Kerala's spice trade, establishing Fort Kochi and leaving behind architectural marvels including churches and the Jewish Synagogue.

1750

Marthanda Varma's Reign

Marthanda Varma of Travancore modernizes the kingdom, strengthens naval power, and dedicates the famous Padmanabhaswamy Temple, establishing Travancore as a major power.

1792

Tipu Sultan's Invasions

Tipu Sultan of Mysore invades Malabar, leading to significant political and social changes. His defeat marks the beginning of British dominance in the region.

1956

Formation of Kerala State

The modern state of Kerala is formed by uniting Malayalam-speaking regions of Travancore, Cochin, and Malabar, creating a unified cultural and political identity.
